Convincing Virtual Reality: Sound, Haptics & Inertia

Full dexterity over the protagonists hands, as made possible in Virtual Reality (VR), completely reinvents traditional gameplay. Interacting with your hands comes very naturally to us, and so the gameplay programmer can spend much fewer iterations interpreting how the player wants to interact with the world. Instead, you get to really hone in on making a truly immersive and responsive world!

A physics-driven lantern.

Whilst taking the bow from inception to completion, we continuously collaborated across disciplines to make repetitive actions like drawing and shooting incredibly fullfilling. Working alongside our audio partner Two Feathers, we provide dynamic feedback down to the creak of the wood as an arrow is drawn. This was then combined with animated bow limbs and controller haptics that respond to the rate of draw back. The result was a bow that changed from being a 'one-of-many' consumable into a permanent sidearm with various flavours of explosive tip ammunition.

Gesture Based Magic

The solution we landed on for reliably detecting gestures was to split detection into both a composition requirement and a motion requirement. The former focused on the relative positions and rotations of the hands, and acted as a prerequisite for the latter. This proved especially useful as it enabled us to inform the player if they have sufficient mana before commiting to any given motion. This crucially prevented frustration during tense moments of combat.

Push, Crush and Lift; the three magic gestures.

From Flicks and Flails, to Full Body Combat

For as long as VR sword fighting has existed, players have fallen into a trap in which they discover that rapid, chaotic flailing is ideal. This could be considered fairplay as it does use up some real life stamina. However, as we are biologically inclined to conserve energy and optimize for laziness, we soon stop moving the arm at all, and simply flick the wrist. The player has unintenionally optimized the fun out of the game, and the compelling axe-wielding fantasy which the player was sold on had quickly dissolved.

In order to give a sense of mass to held weapons, we began by simulating their inertia. This came with a plethora of additional benefits, from limiting the feasibility of weapon flailing to filtering out unintentional muscle spasms. In fact, what started as a quality of life improvement ended up becoming a core system in the game. We split weapons into three classes based on their unique feel: Swords, Axes and Clubs. Swords, the lightest and most aerodynamic class, worked well with just a small dose of a simple dampened oscillator. However, as the effect became more saturated for the heavier classes and the weapon began to trail more and more behind the controller, the effect lost it's charm. To remedy this, we switched to fine-tuned velocity based oscillators for the axe and the club.

Comparing the feel of different weapons.

Finally, we incentivized a more fullfilling playstyle with the introduction of combos. Weapons you find in the world come with a collection of available combos to perform. Whilst completely optional, performing a combo delivers a meaningful damage multiplier. As longer combos are easier to perform with lighter weapons, we ended up designing sets of combos that were tailored to be satisfying and unique for each of the weapon classes.

A combat style which combines combos and magic.
Status Effects for Systemic Gameplay

We originally added the status effect system as a means for spells to have a greater effect beyond just damage. With each status effect providing it's own unique flavour: Ice slows enemies movement and attacks, Fire deals damage over time, Electricity causes enemies to flinch, and so on, players can lean towards whichever flavour suits their current playstyle. This system quickly spread into all forms of combat, including the explosive arrow tips and combo rewards. With the ability to upgrade the damage of your chosen status effects, this became one of our most powerful tools for making each run feel unique.
